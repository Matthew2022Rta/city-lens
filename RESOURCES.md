## Phone numbers & orgs (talk to a real person)

### Legal Aid Society — Housing Help
- **Main:** (212) 577-3300
- Borough offices: Manhattan 212-426-3000 · Brooklyn 718-722-3100 · Bronx 718-991-4600 · Queens 718-286-2450 · Staten Island 347-422-5333
- Free legal representation for tenants. Help in many languages; no immigration status check.
- Note: if you already have an eviction case in housing court, you don't need to call first — go to the **Right to Counsel Help Center** at your borough's housing court (see below).

### Met Council on Housing — Tenants' Rights Hotline
- **Phone:** (212) 979-0611
- **Hours:** Mon 1:30–8:00pm · Wed & Fri 1:30–5:00pm
- Free advice on rights, repairs, and rent; referrals to tenant lawyers and organizing groups.

### Homebase — Eviction Prevention (citywide)
- **Call 311** and say **"Homebase"** to reach your nearest office (offices in all 5 boroughs).
- Free help to prevent eviction and homelessness: rent arrears help, applications, case support.
- (This replaced the single BronxWorks listing — 311/Homebase is citywide and always routes to the right local office.)

### NYC Tenant Helpline / 311
- **Call 311** (say "Tenant Helpline") — housing questions & complaints. 24/7, 200+ languages.

### PATH — family shelter intake (if you have nowhere to stay tonight)
- **Address:** 151 East 151st Street, Bronx, NY 10451
- **Phone:** (718) 503-6400 · **Open 24/7**, including weekends/holidays
- Intake for families with children and pregnant people. NYC has a legal right to shelter.

## Facts the app states — verified

### Notice to Cure / facing eviction
- A notice is **not** an eviction. Only a judge can order an eviction, after a court case. ✅
- A Notice to Cure typically gives **10 days** to fix the claimed lease violation. ✅

### Right to Counsel (free lawyer)
- NYC tenants **with a housing court case** may get a free lawyer. ✅
- Income limit: at or below **200% of the Federal Poverty Level** (~$29,160 for 1 person; ~$60,000 for a family of 4 — adjusts yearly).
- No asset test. Immigration status doesn't disqualify you. No current lease required.
- Covers nonpayment, holdover, and NYCHA termination cases.
- **How:** go to the Right to Counsel Help Center at your borough's housing court, ideally before your first court date.
- ⚠️ Reality check: not everyone eligible actually gets a lawyer — demand exceeds supply. Apply early.

### One-Shot Deal (emergency rent help — HRA)
- Emergency money to cover **rent arrears** and prevent eviction. ✅
- **Apply** via **ACCESS HRA** (online) or in person at an HRA Benefits Access Center. An interview is required. ✅
- Decision can take **30–45 days**; payment usually goes straight to the landlord or housing court.
- Usually requires a current lease (or agreement to stay) and enough income to pay rent going forward.
- ⚠️ Most people must repay at least part of it (SSI recipients generally don't).

### Unsafe conditions
- Heat & hot water are a legal right. **Heat season is Oct 1 – May 31** with minimum temperature rules. ✅
- **Call 311** to file a complaint and create an official record.
- Landlord retaliation for reporting is illegal.

### Affordable housing
- **NYC Housing Connect** (nyc.gov/housingconnect) — apply to affordable apartment lotteries. Free.
- NYCHA & Section 8 have waitlists — get on them as early as possible.
- Never pay a fee to enter a housing lottery.
